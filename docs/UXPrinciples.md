# UX Principles

> **People don't buy AI.**
>
> **People don't buy reports.**
>
> **People buy confidence in important life decisions.**
>
> Every screen, every renderer, every CTA, and every premium feature should help them feel more informed, more confident, and more understood.

## 1. Sell Questions, Not Features
Users don't wake up wanting a "Premium Palm Report". They wake up asking: "Will I be successful?" or "When will I get married?"
- Organize the report around **Your Biggest Questions** rather than generic categories.

## 2. Insight Cards over Long Text
Instead of monolithic text blocks, present findings as digestible **Insight Cards**.
- The free report shows a few Highlight cards.
- Locked cards show specific teaser value (e.g., *"Financial opportunities appear unusually strong. Unlock."*)

## 3. Tangible Metrics (Palm Health Meter)
People love scores. Give the reading a tangible feeling:
- **Overall Score**: 87 / 100
- **Palm Strength**: ★★★★★
- **Life Energy**: ★★★★☆

## 4. The Curiosity Gap (Detected vs Hidden)
Visually quantify exactly what the user is missing to drive conversions.
- Show **Detected vs Hidden** metrics (e.g., *"63 Features Detected. 11 Explained. 52 Still Hidden."*)
- Use **Progress Psychology** (e.g., *"Knowledge Unlocked: 24%"*).

## 5. Trust Through Value (30/70 Rule)
Never cheat the free user. Provide roughly **30% visible value** to build genuine trust, leaving **70% hidden** to motivate the purchase.

## 6. The "Rare Discovery" Hook
If the engine detects an uncommon feature, highlight it aggressively (ensuring the rarity metric is grounded in real rules/data).
- *⭐ Rare Palm Pattern Found. Only 8% of analyzed palms show this pattern. Unlock explanation.*

## 7. Personalized Calls-to-Action
Do not use a generic "Upgrade" button. Use dynamic CTAs based on the user's specific inference result:
- *"You have 17 career insights waiting."*
- *"Your strongest area is Business Success. Unlock the complete Business chapter."*

## 8. General Conversion Rules
- **Never overwhelm the user.**
- **Show the answer before the explanation.**
- **Show evidence only when requested** (The "Why did we predict this?" premium feature).
- **Every locked section must communicate why it's valuable.**
- **Every CTA must emerge naturally from the user's own analysis.**
- **Every premium feature must feel like a continuation, not a paywall.**
